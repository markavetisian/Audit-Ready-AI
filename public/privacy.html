2. How We Use Your Information

  • To provide and operate the Service, including running compliance scans and generating reports.
  • To process subscription payments via Stripe.
  • To send service-related communications (e.g., scan results, billing notices).
  • To improve the Service and develop new features.
  • To detect, prevent, and respond to fraud, abuse, or security incidents.

3. Data Security

We use commercially reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect your data, including access-controlled infrastructure and encryption in transit (TLS). Account data and the evidence references you add (links, file names, and notes) are stored with our infrastructure providers, who encrypt data at rest as part of their platform-level security controls. OAuth tokens are stored server-side and are never exposed to other users. We minimize the personal data we retain from connected integrations — for example, GitHub scans read repository configuration signals (e.g., whether branch protection is enabled) rather than file contents wherever possible. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

4. Evidence You Add

The Evidence Locker stores the links, file names, and notes you add to support your own compliance tracking — it does not host or store the underlying files themselves. We do not sell or share this content with third parties. You are responsible for not including unnecessary personal data (e.g., employee SSNs, passwords) in the references you add — only add what's needed to demonstrate a control.

5. Third-Party Service Providers

We use the following processors to operate the Service:

  • Stripe — payment processing
  • Upstash — encrypted data storage (Redis)
  • Vercel — hosting and infrastructure
  • Groq — AI model inference for report and policy generation (no data retained for training)
  • GitHub, Google — authentication and integration scanning, per the scopes you authorize
  • Slack — account sign-in only (not scanned)

These providers are contractually restricted from using your data for any purpose other than providing services to us.

6. Your California Privacy Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

  • Know what personal information we collect, use, and disclose.
  • Delete your personal information, subject to certain exceptions. You can request deletion at any time from your account dashboard ("Delete Account") or by emailing us — see Section 7.
  • Correct inaccurate personal information.
  • Opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information — we do not sell or share your data, so there is nothing to opt out of.
  • Non-discrimination — we will not deny you service or charge different prices for exercising these rights.

To exercise any of these rights, email support.generalmail@gmail.com. We will verify your identity before fulfilling requests.

7. Data Retention & Account Deletion

We retain your account and compliance data for as long as your account is active. You can permanently delete your account and all associated data (controls, evidence references, reports, score history) at any time, immediately and without contacting us, using the "Delete Account" option in your account menu. You may also request deletion by emailing support.generalmail@gmail.com. We retain backup copies for a limited period after deletion only as needed for legal, billing, or fraud-prevention purposes, after which they are purged.

8. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to individuals under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

9. European & UK Users (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, we process your personal data as a data controller under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and UK GDPR. Our legal bases for processing are: performance of a contract (operating your account and the Service), legitimate interests (security, fraud prevention, service improvement), and consent (where you connect optional integrations). You have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, or port your data, and to object to certain processing — see Section 6 for how to exercise these rights, which we extend to all users regardless of location. Where we engage a processor (Section 5) to handle EEA/UK personal data, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses or an equivalent safeguard for any transfer outside the EEA/UK. A Data Processing Addendum (DPA) is available on request by emailing support.generalmail@gmail.com. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

10. International Users

Our infrastructure is hosted in the United States. By using the Service, you consent to the transfer and processing of your data in the United States, subject to the safeguards described in Section 9 for EEA/UK users.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of material changes via the Service or email.
